\item{\textbf{Question 2.81:}}\\

For what value of k is the following function continuous at the given point.\\

\[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
kx+1 & x \le 5\\
3x-5 & x > 5\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

\textbf{Solution:}\\

\[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
kx+1 & x \le 5\\
3x-5 & x > 5\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\


Since $kx+1$ and $3x-5$ are linear polynomials(always continuous).So, for function f(x) to be continuous, it should be continuous at $x=5$.\\

So,\\

\hspace{3.8 cm}$lim_{x\to5^{+}}f(x)=3(5)-(5)=10$.\\

Also, \hspace{3 cm}$lim_{x\to5^{-}}f(x)=k(5)+1$.\\

Since for continuity,\\

\hspace{3.8 cm}$lim_{x\to5^{+}}f(x)=lim_{x\to5^{+}}f(x)$.\\

or, \hspace{3.3 cm}$10=5k+1$.\\

Hence, \hspace{3.5 cm}$k=9/5$.\\

\item{\textbf{Question 2.82:}}\\

Prove that the function f given by $f(x) = |x - 1|$ , $x \in R$ is not differentiable at $x = 1.$\\

\textbf{Solution:}\\

Here, \hspace{2 cm}$f(x) = |x - 1|$, \hspace{3 cm}$x \in R$\\

\[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
x-1 & x \ge 1\\
-x+1 & x < 1\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

\[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
1 & x \ge 1\\
-1 & x > 1\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

Since at $x=1$ LHS \neq RHS (i.e,1 \neq -1).\\

f(x) is not differentiable at $x=1$.\\

\item{\textbf{Question 2.83:}}\\

Prove that the greatest integer function defined  by $f(x)=[x],0<x<3$ is not differentiable at $x=1$ and $x=2$.\\

\textbf{Solution:}\\

Since,\\

$f(x)=[x]$, $0<x<3$\\

\[f(x)=
\left\{\begin{array}{11}
0 & 0 < x < 1\\
1 & 1 \le x < 2\\
2 & 2 \le x < 3\\
\end{array}
\right. \]\\

$for x=1:$\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}$ lim_{x\to1{-}f(x)=0$.\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}$ lim_{x\to1{+}f(x)=1$.\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}$ lim_{x\to1{-}f(x) \neq lim_{x\to1{+}f(x)$.\\

f(x) is not continuous at 1.\\
 
Similarly,\\

$for x=2:$\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}$ lim_{x\to2{-}f(x)=1$.\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}$ lim_{x\to2{+}f(x)=2$.\\

\hspace{3.5 cm}$ lim_{x\to1{-}f(x) \neq lim_{x\to1{+}f(x)$.\\

f(x) is not continuous at 2.\\

Since, f(x) is not differentiable at x=1 and x=2. So, it is not differentiable at x=1 and x=2.$\\
